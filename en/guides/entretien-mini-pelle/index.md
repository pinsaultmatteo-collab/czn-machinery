# Mini excavator maintenance: the practical guide

Oil changes, filters, greasing, tracks, hydraulics: the complete maintenance schedule to make your mini excavator last 8,000 to 12,000 hours.

⏱ 12 min read
✓ CZN Machinery advice
↻ Updated 2026

Regular maintenance is essential to maximise your mini excavator 's lifespan and avoid costly breakdowns. A well-maintained machine lasts 8,000 to 12,000 hours — 15 to 20 years at 500 to 600 hours a year. A neglected one, by contrast, can suffer an unrepairable failure as early as 1,500 to 2,000 hours.

The cost of well-kept maintenance over 3 years is around €500 to €900 in parts — compared with the €5,000 to €15,000 a major failure (hydraulic pump, engine) can cost.

## Maintenance schedule by interval

### Before each use (daily)

- Engine oil level: check with the dipstick, top up if needed

- Hydraulic oil level: tank sight glass or dipstick

- Fuel level: never run it dry (risk of air in the circuit)

- Coolant: when cold, in the expansion tank

- Track tension: a track that is too loose can come off

- Visible hydraulic hoses: no leaks or abrasion

- Safety devices: seat and arm lock working

### Every 8 to 10 hours

- Grease the arm joints (boom, dipper, bucket — EP2 lithium grease)

- Grease the blade joints

- Grease the slew ring (rotation bearing)

### Every 50 hours

- Check/clean the air filter

- Check the alternator belt (tension, cracks)

- Play in the pivot pins

- Tightness of the track shoe bolts

- Hydraulic filter clogging indicator

### Every 100 hours

- Drain the water/fuel separator

- Check the valve clearance (Laidong, KOOP engines)

- Check the hydraulic pressure

### Every 200 to 250 hours

- Full engine oil change (warm) + oil filter replacement

- Replace the fuel filter and air filter

- Clean the radiator with compressed air (reverse direction)

### Every 500 hours

- Replace the hydraulic filter (return + pilot)

- Change the oil in the travel and slew reduction gears

- Full inspection of the hydraulic hoses

### Every 1,000 hours (or annually)

- Full hydraulic oil change + tank flush

- Replace the coolant

- Check the welds and structure (arm, boom, chassis)

- Overhaul the cylinders (rod seals)

## Common problems and troubleshooting

### Engine overheating

Stop the machine immediately if the temperature exceeds the warning threshold. Common causes: low coolant level, clogged radiator, broken/loose fan belt, faulty thermostat, or prolonged full-power use in hot weather (take breaks every 2 h in summer).

### Hydraulic leaks

Oil stains, a dropping level or loss of power. Causes: a cylinder rod seal, a damaged hose ( replace immediately — risk of high-pressure spray), a loosened/cracked fitting, or a pump seal.

### Starting difficulties

Flat battery (the no. 1 cause, especially in winter), clogged fuel filter, air in the circuit (bleed it), faulty glow plugs, or oil that is too thick (switch to 5W-30/10W-30 below 0 °C).

### Loss of digging power

Check, in order: hydraulic oil level, working pressure, filter condition. A pressure below 200 bar points to a pump or relief-valve problem.

## Winterising and storage

For storage of more than 4 weeks:

- Full engine oil change (used oil is acidic and attacks the seals)

- Check the antifreeze (−25 °C protection recommended)

- Fill the fuel tank to 100% (prevents condensation)

- Grease all joints generously

- Retract the cylinder rods (anti-corrosion)

- Set the machine on blocks (relieves the tracks)

- Disconnect the battery or use a trickle charger

- Cover with a breathable tarpaulin

On restart: reconnect the battery, check the levels, let it idle for 5 minutes, then make slow arm movements to warm up the oil.

## Track maintenance

Rubber tracks are wear parts (1,500 to 3,000 hours depending on the ground). A correctly tensioned track has 10 to 15 mm of sag at the lowest point. Adjust via the greasing valve (add to tighten, bleed to loosen). Wear indicators: lugs under 5 mm, deep longitudinal cracks, visible steel cords (urgent replacement). Cost of a pair: €300 to €800 depending on size.

## Hydraulic circuit maintenance

Rigorous monitoring prevents 80% of hydraulic failures. The oil should stay clean and light amber : black = overheating/mixing, milky = water present. If you hear knocking (cavitation) or notice jerky movements, bleed the air by slowly working all movements at idle. A hose in good condition lasts 4 to 6 years: replace any swollen, cracked, abraded or leaking hose, without waiting for it to burst.

## Replacing the filters

Engine oil filter : warm engine, full oil change, unscrew, fit a new lubricated seal, hand-tighten + 3/4 turn (never with a wrench), refill, check for leaks. Air filter : do not use compressed air inside the housing, respect the cartridge orientation. Hydraulic return filter : cold machine, depressurised circuit, lubricated seal, hand-tighten + 1/2 turn, check the level.

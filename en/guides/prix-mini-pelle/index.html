Price of a new mini excavator in 2026

From €4,125 to €45,080 excl. VAT by weight class. Full price list, comparison with European and Japanese brands, and financing simulations.

⏱ 10 min read ✓ CZN Machinery advice ↻ Updated 2026

The price of a new mini excavator varies widely by brand, weight class and equipment. In France in 2026, the range runs from €4,125 excl. VAT for an entry-level micro excavator to over €80,000 excl. VAT for a 5–6 tonne machine from a premium Japanese brand. Here's the full price list and the comparison with traditional brands.

What does a new mini excavator cost in 2026?

For the most common category — 1 to 3 tonne mini excavators — prices from the major European and Japanese brands (Kubota, Yanmar, Caterpillar, Bobcat, Volvo, Komatsu) generally start at €18,000 excl. VAT and reach €55,000 excl. VAT. This gap versus the CZN brands comes from very different cost structures: dealer networks, brand royalties, European labour costs.

The hidden costs at dealerships

Buying from a European-brand dealership often generates extra charges that push up the final bill:

  • PDI fees (pre-delivery preparation): €300 to €800
  • Transport / delivery: €500 to €1,500 depending on distance
  • Mandatory options bundled in "packs": €1,000 to €3,000
  • Financing arrangement fees: €200 to €500
  • Mandatory service contract: €800 to €2,000/year

A machine listed at €30,000 can end up costing €32,000 to €35,000 once all options and fees are added — whereas the CZN Machinery price is all-inclusive.

Price trends from 2020 to 2026

2020–2021: strong post-Covid demand, longer lead times, prices up 15 to 25% over two years. 2021–2022: semiconductor and raw-material crisis, a further 8 to 12%. 2023–2024: stabilisation, lead times back to normal. 2025–2026: a balanced market. CZN prices rose 8 to 12% since 2020, versus 25 to 40% for European brands — widening the competitiveness gap.

How to get the best price?

Choose the right weight class

Don't oversize. A 1.8 tonne covers 85% of general tradespeople's needs. Each extra tonne represents €3,000 to €10,000 of additional investment.

Compare the all-inclusive price

Ask for a detailed quote including delivery, PDI, full warranty, first bucket and documentation. At CZN, everything is included in the displayed price.

Make the most of financing

Financing over 36 or 48 months lets you buy a new, warranted machine for a monthly payment similar to a used one with no warranty.

Plan attachments ahead

A kit (quick hitch + grading bucket + breaker) bought at the same time is often 10 to 15% cheaper than buying them separately.

Order in the low season

The best lead times are in autumn–winter (October to February). For a spring job, order in winter.

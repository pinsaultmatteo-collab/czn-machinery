Two benchmark diesel engines power mini excavators. We compare origin, reliability, maintenance, parts and cost — with no bias. At CZN, both are available.

On a mini excavator, the diesel engine is the heart of the machine: it drives the hydraulic pump and dictates power, fuel use and longevity. Two names come up again and again when comparing mini excavators: Laidong and Kubota . This guide compares both engines objectively, without favouring either. The good news: at CZN Machinery, mini excavators come fitted as standard with a Laidong engine, and Kubota is available to order — so you don't have to pick your supplier based on the engine.

## Laidong and Kubota: two benchmarks in compact diesel

### The Laidong engine

Laidong is a Chinese engine maker specialising in compact diesel engines (single and twin-cylinder) for agriculture and light construction machinery. On CZN mini excavators from 1.25 to 2 tonnes you'll find the Laidong KM385B twin-cylinder, Euro 5 standard : it delivers more torque and smoothness than a single-cylinder from the class below, for more fluid movements and better performance under load on long days.

### The Kubota engine

Kubota is a Japanese manufacturer and a global benchmark for compact diesel engines . It powers a large share of European and Japanese mini excavators. Its reputation rests on decades of reliability, a worldwide parts network and proven longevity. It's the industry's "premium" engine, with a higher cost that carries through to the final price of the machine.

## Reliability and lifespan

Kubota remains the historical benchmark for reliability and longevity — a strong argument for intensive daily use. For its part, the modern Euro 5 Laidong has improved markedly: simple, robust mechanics that are easy to service, it comfortably handles regular use by a private owner, tradesperson or landscaper. In both cases, lifespan mostly comes down to maintenance : a neglected machine wears out fast whatever the engine brand, while a well-serviced engine lasts several thousand hours.

## Maintenance and spare parts

The maintenance tasks are identical for both engines: oil change, oil filter, air filter, fuel filter, cooling check. The difference is in the parts: Kubota benefits from a very dense global network (but higher prices), while Laidong parts are available at a moderate cost — at CZN, the France-based aftersales service supplies consumables and wear parts. ## Impact on the machine price

The engine choice directly affects the final price. A Kubota makes the machine more expensive : it's a premium component, with the brand value that goes with it. Laidong keeps the price highly competitive at comparable performance for most uses. For a private owner or tradesperson working a few dozen days a year, Laidong offers the best value for money. For a professional in intensive use who prioritises the brand and resale value, Kubota makes sense.

## Our verdict

There's no "bad" engine here. Kubota is the premium benchmark: proven reliability, global network, strong brand value — ideal for intensive use and resale. Laidong offers the best value for money : modern, robust and economical to buy and maintain, it perfectly covers the needs of the vast majority of users.
